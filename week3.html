Cinch Sac Project Overview

The cinch sac is your major project for this unit. You'll create a real, functional drawstring bag you can actually use — to carry your books, gym clothes, or everyday essentials.

Today's job is to plan your design and cut your fabric pieces accurately. Getting this right sets up everything for Weeks 4 and 5. Take your time measuring — small errors now become big problems later!

Your bag will have three main fabric pieces plus two drawstring cords. You can use one fabric for everything or mix a main fabric with a contrasting casing.

Lesson Plan (40 Minutes)
0:00 – 0:07

Project Introduction & Design Planning

Your teacher will show a completed cinch sac and "deconstruct" it — talk through what parts you can see. Then you'll sketch your bag design in your journal: fabric colour, any decoration you plan, why you chose your fabric. Think about: what makes a good book bag? (Strong seams, comfortable strap, easy to open.)

0:07 – 0:15

Fabric Types Recap + Selection

Quick review of Week 1 fabric notes — which fabrics work best for a bag? Look at the Fabric Selector below. Choose your main fabric and, if you want, a contrasting fabric for the casing strip. Bring your choices to the teacher for a quick thumbs-up before cutting.

0:15 – 0:35

Measuring & Cutting

Watch the teacher demonstrate: fold fabric → use a ruler from the selvedge edge → mark with chalk → cut on the line. Then you'll measure and cut your own pieces. Label everything with a fabric marker and clip together. Teacher will circulate to check your accuracy before you proceed.

Which Fabric Should I Choose?
⭐ Best for Beginners

Cotton Canvas

Durable, holds its shape, easy to sew straight seams. The ideal choice for a bag project — sturdy enough to be useful, not too heavy for the machine.

✓ Recommended choice
✓ Also Great

Quilting Cotton

Comes in loads of fun prints and colours! Slightly lighter than canvas but still easy to sew. Great if you want your bag to be colourful and personal.

Good for beginners too
⚠️ More Challenging

Fleece

Soft and cosy, and it doesn't fray! But it's stretchy, which makes it trickier to sew straight. Best if you felt confident with the bookmark.

Ask teacher first
How to Cut Your Fabric
01

Fold & Smooth

Fold your fabric in half, selvedge edges together. Smooth flat on the table — no wrinkles!

02

Measure From Edge

Using a ruler, measure from the selvedge edge inward. Mark your measurement with chalk at two points.

03

Connect the Dots

Use the ruler to draw a straight chalk line connecting your marks. This is your cut line.

04

Cut & Label

Cut along the chalk line. Label each piece with fabric marker. Clip all pieces for the same bag together.

✂️

Cutting Safety Rules

  • Fabric scissors are for fabric ONLY — never paper
  • Keep fingers curled under while cutting
  • Always cut away from your body
  • Fabric must be flat on the table before you cut
  • Pass scissors handle-first to others
