What is an SVG?
🖼️

Regular Image (JPG/PNG)

Made up of tiny coloured squares called pixels. When you zoom in, it gets blurry because you're stretching those squares. Think of it like a mosaic — up close you see all the tiles.

Fine for photos and social media. Not ideal for cutting machines.

⚠️ Gets blurry when enlarged
📐

SVG — Vector Graphic

Made of mathematical paths and shapes — not pixels. When you scale it up, the computer just recalculates the paths, so it stays perfectly sharp at any size. It's like drawing with geometry!

Perfect for Cricut — the machine follows the paths to cut exact outlines.

✓ Always crisp — any size
Sublimation vs Heat-Transfer Vinyl (HTV)

There are two common ways to add designs to fabric using a Cricut and heat. Each works differently and has different results:

🖨️ Sublimation Printing ✂️ Heat-Transfer Vinyl (HTV)
How It WorksSpecial ink turns into a gas and bonds into the fabric fibres permanentlyVinyl is cut by the Cricut, then pressed onto the fabric with heat
Feel on FabricNo texture at all — it becomes part of the fabric itselfSlightly raised and smooth — you can feel the edge
Best FabricWhite or light-coloured polyester onlyAny colour, most fabric types work
ColoursFull-colour photos and gradients — unlimitedOne solid colour per layer of vinyl
DurabilityVery durable — won't crack or peelDurable if applied at the right temperature; can crack if overheated
Cricut's RoleCricut not always needed — prints the design, heat press applies itCricut cuts the vinyl shape; heat press irons it onto fabric

Canva Tips for Cricut Designs

1
Use the Elements Tab

Search for shapes, icons, and graphics in Canva's Elements panel. Use vector-style elements, not photos.

2
Think Bold & Simple

Imagine your design cut out of vinyl. Fine lines will tear. Stick to shapes with a width of at least 0.25 inches.

3
No Photo Backgrounds

Avoid JPG images in your design — the Cricut can't "cut" pixels. Use Canva's flat graphic elements instead.

4
Export Correctly

Share → Download → File Type: SVG (Canva Pro) or PNG. Name the file exactly as instructed before saving.
