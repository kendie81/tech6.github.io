Know Your Machine
1
Presser Foot
Holds fabric flat against the feed dogs while stitching
2
Needle
Pierces fabric to form stitches — always handle with care
3
Bobbin & Case
Holds the lower thread that locks with the top thread
4
Spool Pin
Holds the thread spool at the top of the machine
5
Tension Dial
Controls how tightly the top thread is pulled through
6
Feed Dogs
Metal teeth that move fabric through the machine
7
Stitch Length
Controls how long each individual stitch is
8
Reverse Button
Sews backwards to lock stitches at start and end
9
On/Off Switch
Never turn on until your teacher gives the signal
10
Foot Pedal
Press gently — the harder you press, the faster it goes
⚠️

Safety Rule #1

Never plug in or turn on a sewing machine until your teacher gives the signal. Always keep fingers away from the needle area when the machine is on.

Fabric Explorer

Cotton

Soft · breathable
Clothing, quilts, bookmarks — great for beginners!

Fleece

Fluffy · stretchy
Bags, scarves, blankets — doesn't fray at edges!

Denim

Heavy · stiff
Jeans, bags, aprons — very durable and long-lasting

Satin

Shiny · slippery
Formal wear, linings — can be tricky to sew

Canvas

Heavy · structured
Bags, outdoor projects — holds its shape very well

Felt

Dense · flat
Crafts, decorations — non-woven so it never frays

Jersey Knit

Stretchy · soft
T-shirts, activewear — needs a special stretch needle

Linen

Textured · crisp
Summer clothing, bags — wrinkles easily but breathes well
