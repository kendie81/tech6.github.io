Threading the Drawstring Cord — 4 Steps
01

Attach a Safety Pin

Clip a large safety pin to one end of your first drawstring cord. This is your "needle" — it guides the cord through the casing tunnel.

02

Thread Through

Insert the safety pin into one casing opening and scrunch the fabric to push it all the way around the bag. Pull it back out the same side opening.

03

Repeat with Cord 2

Thread the second cord through the opposite side opening. It travels all the way around and exits the same side. Both cords now form an X inside the casing.

04

Knot & Test

Tie a large double knot at both ends of each cord to stop them pulling through. Pull both cords to test that the bag opens and closes smoothly!

💡

Why two cords? Each cord pulls one side of the bag closed. When you pull both at once, the bag cinches tight. If the cord won't feed through, make sure the casing stitching didn't accidentally close the side openings.

Laundry Symbols — What Do They Mean?

Every piece of fabric has a care label. Always check it before washing anything new — it's the most important step!

🪣
Wash Tub
Machine wash — the number inside = temperature in °C. A hand = hand wash only.
30°C cool · 40°C warm · 60°C hot
🔺
Triangle
Bleaching instructions. Empty = any bleach OK. Lines inside = non-chlorine only.
X through it = no bleach at all
🔲
Square
Drying instructions. Circle inside = tumble dry. Dots = heat level.
1 dot = low · 2 dots = medium
🌡️
Iron
Ironing temperature. Dots show how hot to set the iron.
● = low · ●● = medium · ●●● = high
⭕
Circle
Dry cleaning only. Letters inside tell the cleaner which solvents to use.
X through circle = do not dry clean
Sorting Your Laundry

Sort by Colour

Whites
Lights
Darks

Darks can bleed dye onto lighter fabrics in a hot wash. Turn dark items inside-out before washing to protect the colour.

Sort by Weight

Delicates
Regular
Heavy

Heavy items like denim can damage delicate fabrics. Delicates need a gentle cycle — ideally in a mesh laundry bag.

Before Every Wash — Quick Checklist

✓ Check all pockets
✓ Zip all zippers
✓ Turn dark items inside-out
✓ Read the care label on new items
✓ Treat stains before washing
✓ Don't overload the machine
