Meet the Seam Ripper
🔪

Every Sewist's Best Friend

The seam ripper removes stitches when you need to start over. Don't be afraid to use it — even professional sewists rip seams all the time. It's not failure, it's part of the process!

Slide the hook under a stitch, push forward gently, and the thread pops. After ripping, remove all loose threads before sewing again.

💡 The red ball on the end protects your fabric while the hook does the cutting work.
Sewing the Bag Body — 3 Steps
01

Pin & Prepare

Place your two main bag pieces together, right sides facing each other. Pin down both long sides about every 3 inches. Remove pins as you sew — never sew over them.

If using one folded piece, fold right sides together and pin both open side edges.
02

Sew the Seams

Sew down both long sides using a ½″ seam allowance. Use the edge of the presser foot as your guide. Backstitch at both ends of every seam — this stops your bag from coming apart!

Sew a second line of stitching ¼″ from the first for extra strength.
03

Press Seams Open

At the ironing station, open the seam and press it flat with the iron. This makes the bag lie smoother and look much more professional. Always use the pressing cloth!

Ask the teacher to check your seams before moving to the casing step.
Attaching the Drawstring Casing — 4 Steps

The casing is the channel your drawstring cord will run through. Follow these steps carefully!

1

Fold & Press the Casing

Take each casing strip and fold it in half lengthwise, wrong sides together (so the "good" side shows on both faces). Press the fold flat with the iron.

2

Align & Pin

Place the casing on the bag with raw edges matching at the top. Right sides of casing and bag body should be facing each other. Pin all the way across, making sure the casing lies flat.

3

Sew Across the Top

Sew across the top using a ½″ seam, backstitching at both ends.

⚠️ Leave the short ends of the casing OPEN — this is where the cord will exit the bag!
4

Fold Up & Top-Stitch

Fold the casing up and over the seam allowance to cover the raw edges. Press flat. Top-stitch the bottom edge of the casing down to secure it.

💡 The cord openings should be at each side seam — double-check before you sew!

Common Problems & Fixes
🧵

Thread keeps breaking

The tension may be too tight, or the machine isn't threaded correctly in one spot.

→ Re-thread completely from scratch and check your bobbin
🌀

Thread tangling underneath

Usually means the top thread didn't go through the take-up lever properly.

→ Raise the needle fully, then re-thread the top thread
📏

Seams are wavy, not straight

You may be watching the needle instead of the edge guide on the machine.

→ Watch the edge of the presser foot, not the needle
🔧

Stitches look loose or loopy

The tension is probably off, or the bobbin isn't inserted in the right direction.

→ Check bobbin direction and adjust the tension dial slightly
