Safety First

Do not plug in your machine until your teacher checks your threading and gives the signal. Thread with a partner — one watches, one threads — then swap.

Bookmark — Step by Step

Starting fabric: one strip of cotton, 2.5″ × 8″. Follow each step in order!

01
Fold & Pin
Fold strip in half lengthwise, right sides together. Pin along the edge to hold in place.
02
Sew & Backstitch
Sew the long edge and one short end with a ¼″ seam. Backstitch at start and end!
03
Clip Corners
Cut the corners at an angle — close to the stitching but don't snip through it. Reduces bulk when turned.
04
Turn & Press
Turn right-side out with a pencil or turning tool. Push corners sharp. Press flat with the iron.
05
Top-Stitch
Fold open end under ¼″. Top-stitch around the whole bookmark to close and give a neat finish.
Safe Ironing Rules
🧺

Always use a pressing cloth between the iron and your fabric.

⬆️

Never set the iron face-down on fabric — rest it on its heel.

🌡️

Set the iron to match your fabric type — ask if you're unsure.

🪢

Keep the cord away from your body and away from the table edge.

🚫

Never touch the sole plate — it gets extremely hot and will burn skin instantly.

Hand-Sewing a Button — 4 Steps
01

Thread & Knot

Cut about 50 cm of thread. Thread the needle and tie a double knot at the end.

02

Position Button

Place the button where you want it. Push the needle up through the fabric and through a button hole.

03

Sew Through Holes

Pass through all 4 holes at least 3–4 times each, going down through one hole and up through the other.

04

Wrap the Shank

Bring needle between button and fabric. Wrap thread around the stitches 3 times to form a "shank." Knot off at the back.

Self-Assessment — 20% of Your Grade

Checklist 20%

My seams are straight and even throughout

Corners are clipped and the bookmark lies flat when pressed

The button is firmly attached and doesn't wiggle

I backstitched at the beginning and end of every seam

I cleaned up my workspace before leaving

Rubric Quick Reference

Excellent (4): Seams perfectly straight, button secure with shank, bookmark presses flat

Good (3): Mostly straight seams, button attached, minor finishing issues

Developing (2): Some wavy seams, button slightly loose, seams not pressed

Beginning (1): Incomplete seams or button not securely attached
