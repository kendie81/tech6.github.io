Cinch Sac Project Overview

The cinch sac is your major project for this unit. You will create a real, functional drawstring bag you can actually use — to carry your books, gym clothes, or everyday essentials.

Your bag will have three main fabric pieces plus two drawstring cords. You can use one fabric for everything, or mix a main fabric with a contrasting casing strip for a pop of colour.

Remember: small measuring errors now become big sewing problems later. Take your time, and ask your teacher to check your pieces before you cut.

Which Fabric Should I Choose?
⭐ Best for Beginners

Cotton Canvas

Durable, holds its shape, easy to sew straight seams. The ideal choice for a bag — sturdy enough to be useful, not too heavy for the machine.

✓ Recommended choice
✓ Also Great

Quilting Cotton

Comes in loads of fun prints and colours! Slightly lighter than canvas but still easy to sew. Great if you want a colourful, personal bag.

Good for beginners too
⚠️ More Challenging

Fleece

Soft and cosy, and it doesn't fray! But it's stretchy, which makes straight seams trickier. Best if you felt confident with the bookmark project.

Ask your teacher first
How to Cut Your Fabric — 4 Steps
01

Fold & Smooth

Fold your fabric in half, selvedge edges together. Smooth it completely flat on the table — no wrinkles or bumps.

02

Measure From the Edge

Using a ruler, measure from the selvedge edge inward. Mark your measurement with chalk at two separate points.

03

Connect the Marks

Use the ruler to draw a straight chalk line connecting your two marks. This is your cut line — take a moment to check it looks right.

04

Cut & Label

Cut along the chalk line. Immediately label each piece with a fabric marker and clip all pieces for your bag together so nothing gets lost.

✂️

Cutting Safety Rules

  • Fabric scissors are for fabric ONLY — never use them on paper
  • Keep fingers curled under while cutting
  • Always cut away from your body
  • Fabric must be flat on the table before you cut
  • Pass scissors handle-first to others
