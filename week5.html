Threading the Drawstring Cord
01

Attach a Safety Pin

Clip a large safety pin to one end of your first drawstring cord. This is your "needle" — it will guide the cord through the casing tunnel.

02

Thread Through

Insert the safety pin into one casing opening and scrunch the fabric to push it all the way around the bag. Pull it out the same side opening.

03

Repeat with Cord 2

Thread the second cord through the OPPOSITE side opening. It travels all the way around and comes back out the same side. Both cords now form an X inside the casing.

04

Knot & Test

Tie a large double knot at both ends of each cord to stop them pulling through. Pull the cords to test that the bag opens and closes smoothly!

💡

Why two cords? Each cord pulls one side of the bag closed. When you pull both at once, the bag cinches tight. This is why it's called a cinch sac! If the cord won't feed through, make sure the casing stitching didn't close the side openings.

Laundry Symbols — What Do They Mean?

Every piece of fabric has a care label. Before washing anything you've sewn (or bought), check the label first! Here are the most common symbols:

🪣
Wash Tub
Machine wash — the number inside = temperature in °C. A hand = hand wash only.
30°C cool · 40°C warm · 60°C hot
🔺
Triangle
Bleaching instructions. An empty triangle = any bleach OK. Lines inside = non-chlorine only.
X through it = no bleach at all
🔲
Square
Drying instructions. A circle inside = tumble dry. Dots inside = heat level.
1 dot = low · 2 dots = medium
🌡️
Iron
Ironing temperature. Dots show how hot to set the iron.
● = low · ●● = medium · ●●● = high
⭕
Circle
Dry cleaning only. Letters inside tell the cleaner which solvents to use.
X through circle = do not dry clean
Sorting Your Laundry

Sort by Colour

Whites
Lights
Darks

Darks can bleed dye onto lighter fabrics in a hot wash. Keeping them separate prevents your white shirt from turning pink! Turn dark items inside-out before washing to protect the colour.

Sort by Weight

Delicates
Regular
Heavy

Heavy items like denim and towels can damage delicate fabrics in the wash. They also take longer to dry. Delicates (lace, satin, thin cotton) need a gentle cycle and should ideally go in a mesh bag.

Before Every Wash — Quick Checklist

✓ Check all pockets
✓ Zip all zippers
✓ Turn dark items inside-out
✓ Read new items' care labels first
✓ Treat stains before washing
✓ Don't overload the machine
Bag Assessment — 40% of Your Grade

Self-Assessment Rubric

Cutting Accuracy
My pieces were the right size and cut evenly
Bag Body Seams
Seams are straight, reinforced, and fully sewn
Casing & Cord
Casing is evenly attached; cord threads smoothly
Pressing / Finish
All seams pressed; bag lays flat and looks tidy
Overall Function
Bag opens, closes, and holds contents properly

Peer Assessment

Swap bags with a classmate. Give honest, kind feedback using these prompts:

One Specific Compliment
Something you genuinely notice is well done — be specific, not just "it looks nice."
One Specific Suggestion
Something that could be improved — say what and how, in a kind way.
Overall Impression
Would this bag hold up with daily use? Why or why not?
