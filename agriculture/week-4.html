What we're doing today
We're back in our grow space. Plants have now been growing for two weeks since transplanting — today we measure, observe closely, diagnose any problems, apply our second round of fertilizer, and connect our troubleshooting work to the challenges real Canadian farmers face at scale.
Step-by-step  ·  50 minutes
1
Weekly Plant Check & Measurements  8 min
Visit both the grow tower and raised beds. For each plant, measure height from soil to the tallest point, note leaf colour and number of leaves, and check for stress signs. Add your data to the class chart. Sketch your plant's current state in your journal and compare to your Week 2 sketch.
Look for patterns: are grow tower plants taller? Are raised bed plants bushier? These differences will matter in Week 6's final comparison.
2
Diagnosing Plant Problems  10 min
Even careful growers encounter problems. Learn to read what a plant is telling you.
Yellowing leaves
Cause: Nitrogen deficiency or overwatering
Fix: Check drainage; consider fertilizer
Leggy / stretched stem
Cause: Not enough light — plant reaches for it
Fix: Move closer to a light source
Wilting leaves
Cause: Underwatering OR overwatering
Fix: Check soil moisture before acting
Brown leaf edges
Cause: Over-fertilizing or too much direct sun
Fix: Flush soil with water; reduce fertilizer
Your turn: Look at your plants right now. Do any show these symptoms? With a partner, hypothesize a cause and propose a solution. Be ready to share with the class.
3
Canadian Crop Connection — Farmers Face This Too  8 min
The problems you're diagnosing in our classroom garden happen on a massive scale for Canadian farmers. Discussion questions:
How would a Prairie wheat farmer identify nitrogen deficiency across thousands of hectares? (Hint: satellite imaging and soil testing.)
Why might a pulse crop help the cereal crop grown in the same field the following year? Connect to what you learned in Week 3 about nitrogen fixation.
If a forage crop field shows signs of overwatering after heavy rain, what options does a farmer have that we don't in a classroom?
4
Second Fertilizer Application  10 min
Before applying, observe your plants carefully and answer these in your journal:
① Are leaves a deeper green than Week 2? This may indicate the first dose worked.
② Has stem thickness changed? Note rough estimate in mm if possible.
③ Apply at the base — not on leaves. Record: date, amount, observations.
5
Peer Share — Your Best Observation  12 min
Each person shares one observation or challenge from their journal using a sentence frame:
"I noticed that my plant ________, which I think happened because ________."
"Between Week 2 and Week 4, my plant changed by ________. I predict this because ________."
"One challenge I ran into was ________. I tried ________, and next time I would ________."
Classmates respond with: "I noticed something similar…" or "Have you considered…?"
6
Update Class Data Chart  2 min
The Recorder adds today's height and health observations to the class chart. Make sure Week 2 and Week 4 data are both recorded for comparison.
