Sustainable Watering — Why It Matters  10 min
Water is a finite resource. Growing food uses an enormous amount of it — and climate change is making water availability less predictable across Canada.
Hand watering vs. drip irrigation: Hand watering delivers water across the whole soil surface — some evaporates, some runs off before roots can use it. Drip systems deliver water directly to the root zone, using up to 50% less water and reducing leaf disease from wet foliage. Large-scale Canadian farms increasingly use precision irrigation systems guided by soil sensors.
Canadian context: Prairie farmers in Saskatchewan and Alberta face increasing drought risk due to climate change. Water-efficient crops like lentils and durum wheat — which are deep-rooted and drought-tolerant — are becoming more important. Even in wetter regions like New Brunswick, efficient watering reduces runoff that carries fertilizers into waterways.
3
Build a Rain Gauge  8 min
A simple tool that helps you track how much water your plants are actually getting from rain — so you only add what's needed.
1
Cut a clear plastic bottle — keep the straight-sided bottom section.
2
Use a ruler to mark centimetre lines on the outside with a permanent marker.
3
Place it outdoors in an open area, away from overhangs or trees.
4
After rain, read the measurement and record in your journal. Empty before the next rainfall.
Plants typically need about 2.5 cm of water per week. If rain provides 1.2 cm, you supplement with the remaining 1.3 cm. Overwatering wastes resources and risks root rot.

This unit connects to the SGC global competency. In small groups, explore one of these questions and share a 1-minute response:

Food miles
How far does food travel to reach your plate? What impact does that have on carbon emissions and freshness?
🔄
Crop rotation
Why do farmers rotate crops like cereals and pulses year over year? What happens to soil health if they don't?
🪸
Soil as ecosystem
Healthy soil is full of life — bacteria, fungi, earthworms. How does our use of organic vs. synthetic fertilizer affect that ecosystem?
🏘️
Community gardens
What role do community gardens play in food security and neighbourhood connection? Are there any near your school?
