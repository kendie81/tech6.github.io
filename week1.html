Week 1 of 6  ·  Hands-On

From Seed to Plan

Growing Food: Seeds to Harvest  ·  Grade 6–7

● 50 minutes
Learning goals
By the end of this class, I can…
Explain what a seed needs in order to germinate
Save seeds from a mature vegetable and store them correctly
Plant seeds in a seed-starting tray and record my planting data
Compare the grow tower and raised beds and predict one difference
What we're doing today
Today kicks off our 6-week growing journey. We'll explore what seeds really are, save seeds from a real vegetable, plant our first seeds in trays, and take a close look at the two growing spaces we'll use — a grow tower and raised outdoor beds. You'll start your garden journal today and use it every week.
Step-by-step  ·  50 minutes
1
KWL Chart  8 min
In your journal, draw three columns: What I Know, What I Want to Know, What I Learned. Fill in the first two columns independently, then share one item with a partner. Complete the third column at the end of class.
2
Tour — Grow Tower & Raised Beds  7 min
Walk to both spaces. Note three things you observe and one question for each. Think about: how are they different? Which do you predict will grow better — and why?
Sketch a quick diagram of each space in your journal.
3
Seed Dissection  7 min
Peel the seed coat off a soaked bean. Inside, find the two cotyledons and the tiny embryo between them. Sketch and label all three parts in your journal.
A seed has everything it needs to begin life — it just needs water, warmth, and sometimes light to wake up. This is called germination.
4
Seed Saving from a Mature Vegetable  10 min
Follow along with a tomato, pepper, or squash:
① Cut open the vegetable and scoop seeds into a bowl of water.
② Rinse and rub off pulp. Viable seeds sink; empty ones float.
③ Spread on a paper towel — needs at least 24 hours to dry.
④ Once dry, place in seed envelope. Label: variety, date, your name.
5
Plant Seeds in Your Tray  10 min
Choose your variety, then:
① Fill each cell with potting mix — gently packed, not tight.
② Make a hole about as deep as the seed is wide (roughly 1–2 cm).
③ Drop 1–2 seeds in and cover gently with soil.
④ Label each cell. Record in journal: variety, date, tray location.
6
First Watering Practice  5 min
Do the finger test — push your finger 2 cm into the soil. Dry at the tip = needs water. Water slowly and evenly. Overwatering is one of the most common mistakes new growers make.
Record the date and whether you watered in your journal. You'll do this every week.
7
Complete Your KWL Chart  3 min
Fill in the What I Learned column. Aim for at least two things you didn't know before today's class.
