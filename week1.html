Lesson Plan
0:00 – 0:05

Hook & Introduction

Watch a short video clip of a fashion show or a maker creating a bag. Discuss: "Who made the clothes you're wearing today?" View the two finished projects — bookmark and cinch sac. Sign your safety agreement before any equipment is used.

0:05 – 0:15

Sewing Careers Carousel

Explore 12 careers connected to sewing through a slideshow — one slide per career, fast and visual. After the slideshow, pair-share: "Which career surprised you most?"

0:15 – 0:25

Sewing Machine Orientation

Machines are unplugged. Label a printed diagram as your teacher points out each part. Golden rule: NEVER plug in until your teacher says so.

0:25 – 0:35

Fabric Exploration Station

Each table has a kit of 8–10 labelled fabric swatches. Complete your journal chart: fabric name, how it feels, and one guess at how it's used. Your teacher will come around to chat about what you found.

0:35 – 0:40

Exit Ticket & Preview

Write on a sticky note: 3 machine parts you remember + 1 career that interests you and why. Next week: we sew our first project — a bookmark!

Know Your Machine
1
Presser Foot
Holds fabric against the feed dogs while stitching
2
Needle
Pierces fabric to form stitches — always handle with care
3
Bobbin & Case
Holds the lower thread that locks with the top thread
4
Spool Pin
Holds the thread spool at the top of the machine
5
Tension Dial
Controls how tightly the top thread is pulled
6
Feed Dogs
Metal teeth that move fabric through the machine
7
Stitch Length
Controls how long each individual stitch is
8
Reverse Button
Sews backwards to lock stitches at the start and end
9
On/Off Switch
Never turn on until your teacher gives the signal
10
Foot Pedal
Press gently to sew — the harder you press, the faster it goes
Fabric Explorer

Cotton

Soft, breathable
Clothing, quilts, bookmarks — great for beginners!

Fleece

Fluffy, stretchy
Bags, scarves, blankets — doesn't fray!

Denim

Heavy, stiff
Jeans, bags, aprons — very durable

Satin

Shiny, slippery
Formal wear, linings — delicate to sew

Canvas

Heavy, structured
Bags, outdoor projects — holds its shape

Felt

Dense, flat
Crafts, decorations — non-woven, no fraying

Jersey Knit

Stretchy, soft
T-shirts, activewear — needs stretch needle

Linen

Textured, crisp
Summer clothing, bags — wrinkles easily
