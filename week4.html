Warm-Up: Meet the Seam Ripper
🔪

Every Sewist's Best Friend

The seam ripper removes stitches when you need to start over. Don't be afraid to use it — even professional sewists rip seams all the time. It's not a sign of failure, it's part of the process!

Today you'll practice on a pre-sewn sample strip before touching your bag. Slide the hook under a stitch, push forward gently, and the thread pops. After ripping, remove all the loose threads before sewing again.

💡 Pro tip: The red ball on the end protects the fabric while the hook does the cutting work.
Sewing the Bag Body
01

Pin & Prepare

Place your two main bag pieces together, right sides facing each other. Pin down both long sides — about every 3 inches. Remove pins as you sew, never sew over them.

If using one folded piece, fold right sides together and pin both open side edges.
02

Sew the Seams

Sew down both long sides using a ½" seam allowance. The edge of the presser foot = your guide. Backstitch at both ends of every seam — this is what stops your bag from falling apart!

Then sew a second line of stitching ¼" from the first for extra strength.
03

Press Seams Open

At the ironing station, open the seam and press it flat with the iron. This makes the bag lie smoother and look much more professional. Always use the pressing cloth!

Ask the teacher to check your seams before moving to the casing step.
Attaching the Drawstring Casing

The casing is the channel the drawstring cord will run through. Follow these steps carefully — the casing placement must be accurate for the bag to work!

1

Fold & Press the Casing

Take each casing strip and fold it in half lengthwise, wrong sides together (so you can see the "good" side on both faces). Press the fold flat with the iron.

2

Align & Pin

Place the casing on the bag with raw edges matching at the top. Right sides of casing and bag body should be facing each other. Pin all the way across, making sure the casing lies perfectly flat.

3

Sew Across

Sew across the top using a ½" seam, backstitching at both ends. Remove pins as you go.

⚠️ Leave the short ends of the casing OPEN — this is where the cord will exit the bag!
4

Fold Up & Top-Stitch

Fold the casing up and over the seam allowance, so it covers the raw edges inside. Press flat. Then top-stitch the bottom edge of the casing down to secure it. Leave the side openings open for the cord.

💡 The cord openings should be at each side seam — check before you sew!

Always test on a scrap piece of your fabric first. When in doubt, use a lower temperature!

Common Problems & Fixes
🧵

Thread keeps breaking

The tension may be too tight, or the machine isn't threaded correctly in one spot.

→ Re-thread from scratch and check your bobbin
🌀

Thread tangling underneath

Usually means the top thread isn't through the take-up lever properly.

→ Raise the needle fully, re-thread the top thread
📏

Seams are wavy, not straight

You may be watching the needle instead of the edge guide.

→ Watch the edge of the presser foot, not the needle
🔧

Stitches look loose or loopy

The tension is probably off, or the bobbin isn't inserted correctly.

→ Check bobbin direction and adjust tension dial slightly
