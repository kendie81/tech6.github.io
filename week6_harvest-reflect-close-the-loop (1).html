2
Seed Saving from Our Own Harvest  10 min
In Week 1, we saved seeds from a store-bought vegetable. Today we close the loop — saving seeds from plants we grew ourselves. These carry the genetics of our own growing season.
① Choose the healthiest, most mature vegetable from your plant.
② Extract seeds, rinse, and float-test. Good seeds sink.
③ Dry on a paper towel. Label your envelope: variety, date, "grown by [your name]."
④ These seeds go home with you — grow them again next season.
Think about it: Saving seeds from your own plant is the same principle that has sustained farming for thousands of years — and that Canadian farmers still use when selecting seed stock. How does seed saving connect to food independence and the Canadian crop cycle you studied in Week 3?
