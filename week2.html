Safety First

Do NOT plug in your machine until your teacher checks your threading AND gives the signal. Thread with a partner — one watches, one threads. Switch after.

Bookmark — Step by Step

Starting fabric: one strip of cotton, 2.5" × 8". Follow each step in order!

01
Fold & Pin
Fold strip in half lengthwise, right sides together. Pin along the edge to hold in place.
02
Sew & Backstitch
Sew along the long edge and one short end using a ¼" seam. Backstitch at start and end!
03
Clip Corners
Cut the corners at an angle — close to the seam but don't snip through it. This reduces bulk when turned.
04
Turn & Press
Turn right-side out using a pencil or turning tool. Push corners sharp. Press flat with the iron.
05
Top-Stitch
Fold open end under ¼". Top-stitch all the way around to close and give a neat professional finish.
Safe Ironing Rules
🧺

Always use a pressing cloth between the iron and your fabric.

⬆️

Never set the iron face-down on fabric — rest it on its heel.

🌡️

Set the iron to match your fabric type — ask your teacher if unsure.

🪢

Keep the cord away from your body and away from the edge of the table.

🚫

Never touch the sole plate — it gets extremely hot and will burn skin.

Hand-Sewing a Button
01

Thread & Knot

Cut about 50 cm of thread. Thread the needle and tie a double knot at the end.

02

Position Button

Place the button where you want it on the bookmark. Push needle up through fabric and through a button hole.

03

Sew Through Holes

Pass through all 4 holes at least 3–4 times each, going down through one hole and up through the other.

04

Create the Shank

Bring needle between button and fabric. Wrap thread around the stitches 3 times to form a "shank." Knot off at the back.

Assessment — 20% of Your Grade

Self-Assessment Checklist 20%

My seams are straight and even throughout the bookmark

Corners are clipped and the bookmark lies flat when pressed

The button is firmly attached and doesn't wiggle

I used safe machine habits throughout (backstitched, raised needle)

I cleaned up my workspace before leaving

Rubric Quick Reference

Excellent (4): Seams perfectly straight, button secure with shank, bookmark presses flat

Good (3): Mostly straight seams, button attached, minor finishing issues

Developing (2): Some wavy seams, button a bit loose, seams not pressed

Beginning (1): Incomplete seams, button not attached or falling off
