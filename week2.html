What we're doing today
Our seeds from Week 1 should be sprouting! Today we check on our seedlings, compare how the two growing systems work, transplant our strongest plants, apply fertilizer for the first time, and set up our class care schedule and rotating roles.
Step-by-step  ·  50 minutes
1
Germination Check  7 min
Visit your seed tray. Record in your journal: how many cells have sprouted, how tall the seedlings are, and what the first leaves look like. These early leaves are called cotyledon leaves. Sketch what you see.
Leggy, pale seedlings are reaching for more light — note this for troubleshooting in Week 4.
2
Grow Tower vs. Raised Bed — How are they different?  8 min
Study the comparison below, then create your own T-chart prediction in your journal.
FeatureGrow TowerRaised Bed
OrientationVertical — pockets stacked upwardHorizontal — plants spread across the surface
WateringDrips down through all layers from the topWatered across the surface; finger test each visit
Best forHerbs, lettuce, strawberries, compact plantsTomatoes, squash, root vegetables, tall plants
ChallengesTop layers may dry faster; limited root roomMore area to weed and monitor
3
Transplanting Seedlings  10 min
Move the strongest seedlings into the grow tower and raised beds. Roots are fragile — handle carefully.
① Water cells about an hour before transplanting — damp roots handle better.
② Squeeze the bottom of the cell to pop out the root ball. Never pull by the stem.
③ Make a hole slightly larger than the root ball in your growing space.
④ Set the seedling in, fill with soil, press gently around the base.
⑤ Water lightly at the base. Label with variety and your name.
4
Introduction to Fertilizer — N, P, K  8 min
Plants make their own food through photosynthesis, but they need nutrients from the soil to grow strong. Fertilizer replaces nutrients the soil loses over time.
N
Nitrogen
Leafy green growth — bigger leaves need more N
P
Phosphorus
Root development and fruit production
K
Potassium
Overall health, disease resistance, strong stems
Organic vs. synthetic: Organic fertilizers (compost, seaweed, fish meal) release nutrients slowly and improve soil over time. Synthetic fertilizers release nutrients quickly but can damage soil biology if overused.
5
Apply First Fertilizer Dose  7 min
Dilute liquid fertilizer according to package directions and apply to both growing sites.
① Read the label together — how many mL per litre?
② Measure and mix carefully. More is NOT better — too much burns roots.
③ Apply at the base of each plant, not on leaves.
④ Journal entry: date, fertilizer type, dilution rate, observations.
6
Set Up Care Schedule & Roles  10 min
Good growing needs consistent daily care between classes. These four roles rotate each week.
💧
Waterer
Checks moisture and waters daily as needed
👁️
Observer
Records daily changes: growth, colour, problems
📋
Recorder
Updates class data chart with height and notes
🌿
Tower Lead
Daily care responsibility for the grow tower
Write your role for this week in your journal header. You'll rotate so that by Week 6, you've experienced at least two different roles.
